Whisk is a variant of Tic Tac Toe with some important differences. For one thing, it is played on an 8 by 8, rather than a 3 by 3, board.
Players place their icon, either X or O, on the board by selecting a square and clicking on it. They receive points for getting 3, 4, or 5 of their icons in a row: 1 point for 3 in a row, 4 points for 4 in a row, and 9 points for 5 in a row. Neither player can get more than 5 icons in a row due to a unique feature of Whisk: when a player places a sixth icon on the board, the oldest icon of that kind disappears, leaving just the five most recent ones. As a visual reminder of this, the icons fade progressively as new ones are placed.
You are playing the game in remote mode, meaning that the players are on two different computers and in general cannot see each other's screen. Whisk takes advantage of this fact to eliminate the advantage that the first player often enjoys in a game of this kind by having the players move, in effect, simultaneously. The first player to make place an icon sees that move as expected but the second player's screen is not updated until he or she has made a move. If the second player happens to select the same square that the first player chose, that results in an error message and the player must try again.
The game ends when either or both players get 50 or more points. If both players achieve the same score, greater than 50, on the same move, the game is a tie.
