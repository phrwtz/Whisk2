Whisk is a variant of Tic Tac Toe with some important differences. For one thing, it is played on an 8 by 8, rather than a 3 by 3, board.
Players place their icon, either X or O, on the board by selecting a square and clicking on it. They receive points for getting 3, 4, or 5 of their icons in a row: 1 point for 3 in a row, 4 points for 4 in a row, and 9 points for 5 in a row. Neither player can get more than 5 icons in a row due to a unique feature of Whisk: when a player places a sixth icon on the board, the oldest icon of that kind disappears, leaving just the five most recent ones. As a visual reminder of this, the icons fade progressively as new ones are placed.
You are playing the game against the computer; you are playing O, the computer, whose name is WhiskBot, is playing X.
In games of this kind, including the original Tic Tac Toe, the first player often enjoys an advantage. In two-player mode, Whisk minimizes this asymmetry by taking advantage of the fact that the two players are on different computers and cannot see each other's screen. By not revealing their opponent's move until they have made their own, Whisk forces them to move simultaneously. Since you are playing the computer there is only one screen, but Whisk simulates the two-screen condition. This is how that works: if you make a move before WhiskBot does, you see your move on the screen but your move is not revealed to WhiskBot. If WhiskBot moves before you do, you will be informed of that fact but you will not see WhiskBot's move until you make your own. If the second player to move, either you or WhiskBot, happens to select the same square that the first player has already chosen, that results in an error message and the player must try again.
The game ends when either or both players get 50 or more points. If both players achieve the same score, greater than 50, on the same move, the game is a tie.
